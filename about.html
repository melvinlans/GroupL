About Us

About The Founder

Ms.Linda Muckson Sesay

.

Growing up in Sierra Leone we saw the desperate need for quality healthcare for people outside major towns and have dedicated our lives to helping improve the lives of these people. The mission has been to become trained in the medical field. Since earning her LPN, RN, BSN, Linda is a Healthcare professional with 8 years of experience working in the Nursing field in Pediatrics, medical-surgical, Rehabilitation, Geriatric care, and Hospice care. Also, she is an instructor for CPR/First Aid (BLS with the American Heart Association)..

You can help us and the people of Sierra Leone West Africa, by posting our medical center project on social media, donating clothes, furniture or any type of used medical equipment including walkers, wheelchairs, hospital beds, blankets or anything you think we may need. With the help of loving and compassionate people, we will not only achieve or dream of building this first medical clinic but also many other clinics in Sierra Leone and other parts of Africa as we network our skills and abilities with others trying to do the same thing. Together we are stronger, and together as a global family, we can all make a difference to providing loving care to the people of Sierra Leone.

Make A Difference Today

The government of Sierra Leone, through the Ministry of Health and Sanitation, has today the 19th June 2020, received 40 Intensive Care Unit (ICU) bed as a donation from the Community Health Foundation (CHF), a non-profit organization based in Maryland, United States Receiving the donation, the Minister of Health and Sanitation, Dr. Alpha Wurie, thanked the team of the foundation for a patriotic move. He admitted that ” Sierra Leone has only one Intensive Care Unit (ICU) bed, currently at the Connaught Hospital.” He further expressed that “with this donation, the ministry now has enough ICU beds for distribution to form a nucleus for emergency operations across Sierra Leone” In a speech read on her behalf by Mr. Jacob Sax Conteh, the Principal of Canadian College of Modern Technology mile 91, Ms. Linda Sesay, CEO and Founder of CHF, promised to work with the government and private sector to help improve the health care system in Sierra Leone. Community Health Foundation is a 501c(3) non-profit health organization whose sole objective is to provide healthcare service for the less privileged in Sierra Leone.
